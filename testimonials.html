Jenny

The Story about a Journey

This article is rather a story which I would like to share with all of you.

Seven years ago, a girl shifted from her hometown to a new place. She got an admission in a new school, where nobody knew her, where she knew no one. She felt lonely and lost. Everyday she used to go to the school thinking, today she’ll make new friends, but all of them already had enough friends. Except a few, nobody cared to talk to the new girl. During lunch hours she would sit alone in the classroom and think where to go and with whom to share her lunch with. She knew all this was happening because she was not able to interact with the class. She was a little shy to go and ask anyone to show her around the school or take her in their group. So she kept to herself. Slowly and gradually she started talking to 3-4 students in the class.

One day, an announcement was made in the class, during the Accounts period, by the teacher, that a workshop will be conducted every Thursday. There was an air of confusion and chaos, as each one of the students thought it might be very boring, as no one had any clue about what it was going to be.

On the following Thursday that week, the Accounts teacher introduced the class to Mr. Raju Kumar, their Theatre Director! Everyone got excited and thrilled. They didn’t know what was coming ahead, but all they knew was, it’s going to be fun! The Theatre workshops began. Students were set in different small groups always, and were assigned a variety of activities. Slowly and gradually, that girl got to know the names of almost all her classmates. Also they started interacting with her. She made a lot of new friends, now she had lunch with them. She was also introduced to other students in the school, belonging to different streams, like Science. Things were changing for her, she started liking the school and the people around. All the students addressed Mr. Raju as Raju Sir. He told them that soon they would be doing a play which will be enacted for their Accounts subject (School Syllabus). The preparations for the play began. Raju Sir assigned roles and defined characters to all the students. Scripts were written, costumes were designed, dances were choreographed, funds arranged. The show was a huge success, and Raju Sir praised everyone saying this was his best show ever!

This was just the beginning of the Journey. After that show, there was no stopping or looking back, there were numerous trips, indoor and outdoor, more than 25 shows had been performed, they had become so popular, the word was everywhere…in newspapers, television channels etc. All this affected the girl so much, she got this kind of exposure for the first time in life…she was now recognized by so many people, and she felt like coming out of the cocoon into the real world, her morale had been boasted, she was more confident and outspoken now. She didn’t feel shy speaking to anyone in the class or elsewhere, everyone was her friend now.

There was a breaking point in her so called theatre career, when the group was supposed to perform a show at National School of Drama (NSD, Delhi), Raju Sir came up to her and asked her, will you play the main role in the enactment “Jesse jays ki naming?” She was surprised, and obviously said, “Yes Sir.” Her world changed after that. She felt like the queen of the world, because she was given such a big responsibility and a chance to exhibit her talent.

That “Jesse” actually had a makeover then!

That “Jesse” today is narrating this beautiful story to you all…

Settled in Dubai (U.A.E), working as Functional Consultant in IT Software Company, I owe a lot to Raju Sir and all my friends who helped me become what I’m today! To help me bring out the actual JENTI TAHILIANI to you! To never be shy or hesitant about anything! To always give your 100% and be sure of yourself in everything you do! To be confident and last but not the least to work as a Team!

Having an opportunity, I would like to thank, first and foremost, Mr. Raju Kumar and theatre for making Accountancy such an interesting and easy to understand subject.

The thing that changed my life

Often, we meet some people who bring changes in our lives, changes that are good and which stay forever. For me, one such person indeed was Raju Sir, with his magical power of Theatre. Theatre was something that I, for one, never anticipated in my life.

But when it came to theatre, Raju Sir was different. His opinion about theatre is not acting alone; it’s rather putting emotions to actions - for acting, music, dance or script writing. He believed that theatre cannot be taught so he considered himself also not a theatre teacher/instructor but a facilitator. He made us strong through several exercises that focused on self improvement and confidence building. This brought my ‘hidden inside’, out. The change in me was conspicuous to everyone. I am indebted to him for nurturing us with the charm of theatre.

The confidence I gained while working with him, helped me sail through the journey for my aspired college and the dream B school.

College started or rather continued with the same zeal. Theatre was a constant support and motivator. It helped me practice the things learnt in the form of street plays; class presentations; performing on the stage or anchoring. Life is nothing but an experience. His guidance helped me do better at every step and take my experience a step further. Not only had I improved as an artist, my people skills also improved with time.

Raju sir lifted my confidence and inspired me to work with his eternal spirit and love towards theatre.

To sum up, I believe that “cause-effect relationship” is the answer to every question in the world. Call this the cause or the action that I chose this path to contribute in my small way to change the world. Of what life I have seen till now, I have learnt that taking your commitments sincerely is an important thing and that it pays off. I believe that I am here today because I took my commitments sincerely one at a time, and I am grateful to god for that.

I wish the mirror on the wall, helps you to recognize your own reflection. And I can easily assure you, the you are at the right most place for this job.

Chamak us sooraj ki aakhir, kirno se hi hoti hai

Wishing you all the luck in future under my lifetime MENTOR.

Ankush

My life, my identity, my name, “ANKUSH”, was lost somewhere or I was searching for a pathway. Though I was associated with theatre field but still that satisfaction was missing somewhere. It’s not just about working in this field, it’s all about right direction and proper guidance. I realized it when I came in contact with Mr. Raju Kumar and started working with LEELA Group. After being the part of different annual productions, workshops, school based theatre activities and theatre in education programs conducted by Leela Group I have felt a kind of transformation in my personality and my attitude towards life. I am still a learner but now I know where I stand, what are my assets and my potentials. Now I know who ANKUSH is and this confidence has been developed during my journey with leela.

Abhishek Somvansi

To be honest most of my qualities which people acknowledge are bestowed upon me by theatre. For me theatre gives a sense of freedom from our own restrictions and boundaries which we have created around us and help us to unfold the layers of happiness that lies within us….With time I have realized how much a theatre set me on the road towards success. It taught me to value the every single effort of each individual… I have encountered the most amazing and stimulating cross-section of humanity in theatre. The great thing about the stage is that when it’s live and you’re up in front of that audience anything can, and does, happen. Dropped lines, missed entrances, or malfunctioning props require you to improvise while maintaining yours cool. Theatre taught me how to focus, think quickly and make do while giving the impression that you’ve got it all under control. It’s not the road to fortune. Many Plays have shoestring budget. Though its theatre which have taught me how to be imaginative, do more with less and to give my best shot with what I have got. Being a soft skill trainer, and moreover a social being I have to appreciate, understand and effectively communicate with a widely diverse group of social beings with being motivating and a little entertaining that has ultimately made me a better (though far from perfect) mankind in every role which could not be possible without theatre.

Divyank Jain
